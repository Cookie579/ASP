Investment Thesis for Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce (CM)

September 21, 2024

Profile:

Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce is a Canada-based financial institution. The Company has 13 million personal banking, business, public sector and institutional clients. Across personal and business banking, commercial banking and wealth management, and capital markets businesses, the Company offers a full range of advice, solutions and services through its digital banking network and locations across Canada, with offices in the United States and around the world. Its personal banking offers products and services, including bank accounts, credit cards, mortgages, lending, investments, insurance, ways to bank and smart advice. Its business banking products and services include accounts, credit cards, borrowing, investing, cash management, smart business advice and healthcare. It also offers various business solution, including Managing Cash Flow, Financing Your Business and Day-to-Day Banking.

Executive Summary:

Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce (CIBC) (Ticker: CM) remains a key player in the North American banking industry, offering a wide range of financial products and services. As of September 21, 2024, CM is trading at CAD 55.43 per share, having posted a steady YTD return of approximately 8%. Despite ongoing challenges such as rising interest rates, inflation, and regulatory pressure in the global financial markets, CIBC has managed to sustain positive performance metrics. The stock is attractive due to its strong dividend yield of 6.2%, making it an appealing choice for income-focused investors.

CIBC’s current strategic focus on expanding its U.S. business operations, managing capital efficiently, and digital banking initiatives are expected to contribute to its long-term growth. This report delves into the company’s performance, its position in the financial services sector, and the rationale behind its strong future growth potential.

Market Overview:

The financial services industry, particularly banks, has been significantly impacted by global macroeconomic conditions in 2024. Central banks in major economies, including the U.S. Federal Reserve and the Bank of Canada, have continued tightening monetary policies to curb persistent inflation, which has pushed up interest rates to multi-year highs. While these conditions have raised concerns around consumer debt, they have created an environment where banks, including CIBC, have benefitted from higher net interest margins (NIMs) due to increased loan rates.

Comparison with Peers:

Compared to its industry peers, including Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD) and Royal Bank of Canada (RY), CIBC has shown modest but steady growth. Over the past 12 months, CIBC’s stock returned around 8%, while TD and RY posted YTD returns of 6.5% and 7%, respectively, as of mid-September 2024.

  • CIBC (CM): YTD return ~8%, Dividend Yield 6.2%, P/E Ratio: 9.8
  • TD Bank (TD): YTD return ~6.5%, Dividend Yield 4.6%, P/E Ratio: 10.3
  • Royal Bank of Canada (RY): YTD return ~7%, Dividend Yield 4.5%, P/E Ratio: 11.2

CIBC’s dividend yield stands out as one of the highest among major Canadian banks, making it particularly attractive to income-oriented investors. While its P/E ratio is slightly lower than its competitors, this reflects a conservative valuation relative to its earnings, indicating potential upside if the bank meets its earnings growth targets.

Key Growth Drivers:

a) U.S. Expansion Strategy:

CIBC has been increasingly focused on expanding its presence in the U.S. banking market, which currently contributes to about 20% of its revenue. The bank’s acquisition of The PrivateBank in 2017 marked the beginning of its U.S. growth strategy, and since then, CIBC has steadily increased its exposure to the U.S. market, particularly in commercial and wealth management sectors.

The U.S. market provides higher growth opportunities compared to Canada’s mature banking industry. Over the next few years, CIBC plans to further expand its U.S. operations, taking advantage of higher loan demand, stronger GDP growth, and the potential for higher profit margins in its wealth management division.

b) Digital Transformation and Efficiency Initiatives:

CIBC has been making significant investments in its digital infrastructure to modernize its operations and enhance customer experience. The bank’s focus on digital banking has allowed it to reduce operational costs and improve efficiency. The digital transformation has also enabled CIBC to compete more effectively with fintech players, attract tech-savvy younger customers, and offer a seamless online and mobile banking experience.

As of Q3 2024, digital banking penetration at CIBC reached 75%, up from 70% in 2023. This trend is expected to continue, helping the bank to streamline its operations and improve its cost-to-income ratio, which currently stands at 53%.

Rationale and Justification:

Financial Performance:

As of Q3 2024, CIBC reported strong earnings, with a net income of CAD 1.68 billion, a 4.3% increase compared to the same period last year. The bank's total revenue for the quarter stood at CAD 5.68 billion, reflecting steady growth across its core divisions of personal, business, and commercial banking, as well as wealth management.

Key financial metrics:

  • Net Interest Margin (NIM): 2.4% (Q3 2024), up from 2.1% in Q3 2023.
  • Earnings per Share (EPS): CAD 3.81 for Q3 2024, up 5% Y/Y.
  • Return on Equity (ROE): 15.2%, one of the highest among Canadian banks.

Technical Analysis:

From a technical standpoint, CIBC shows positive momentum. As of September 21, 2024, the Relative Strength Index (RSI) for CM stands at 54, indicating that the stock is neither overbought nor oversold, offering room for upward movement. The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) has also recently crossed above the signal line, which is considered a bullish indicator, suggesting that the stock may see further price appreciation.

The stock’s 50-day moving average of CAD 54.22 is slightly below its current price of CAD 55.43, showing that the short-term trend is positive. The 200-day moving average of CAD 52.88 further supports the bullish outlook.

Dividend Appeal:

CIBC’s strong dividend yield of 6.2% (as of September 2024) is another factor that makes this stock attractive, particularly in a high-interest-rate environment where yield-seeking investors are favoring high-dividend stocks. With a conservative payout ratio of 58%, the bank has ample room to maintain or even increase its dividends in the future.

Risks and Headwinds:

Despite its strong financials and growth outlook, CIBC faces several risks that could potentially impact its stock price:

  • Macroeconomic Risks: Rising interest rates, persistent inflation, and the potential for an economic slowdown pose significant risks. An economic recession, particularly in North America, could lead to higher credit losses and a reduction in loan demand.
  • Regulatory Pressure: The banking industry is subject to heavy regulation, and changes in regulatory policies, particularly related to capital requirements or consumer protection, could impact CIBC’s profitability.
  • U.S. Market Risks: While the U.S. expansion provides growth opportunities, it also exposes CIBC to additional risks, including currency fluctuations, geopolitical instability, and increased competition from local U.S. banks.
  • Technological Disruption: The rise of fintech and digital-only banks presents a challenge. Although CIBC has made progress in digital transformation, competition from more nimble fintech players could erode market share.
  • Housing Market Exposure: CIBC has significant exposure to the Canadian housing market, which has shown signs of cooling due to higher mortgage rates. A significant downturn in the housing market could negatively impact its loan portfolio.

Conclusion:

CIBC presents an attractive investment opportunity in the current market environment. With its stable earnings, focus on U.S. expansion, and commitment to digital transformation, the bank is well-positioned for future growth. The stock’s strong dividend yield of 6.2%, coupled with reasonable valuation metrics such as a P/E ratio of 9.8 and an ROE of 15.2%, provides a compelling case for both income and value investors.

However, potential risks such as macroeconomic uncertainty, regulatory challenges, and exposure to the Canadian housing market must be carefully monitored. For investors looking for stability and income in the financial services sector, CIBC remains a solid choice with good growth prospects over the long term.

Investment Thesis for Nasdaq, Inc. (NDAQ)

August 9, 2024

Profile:

Nasdaq, Inc. is a global technology company serving corporate clients, investment managers, banks, brokers, and exchange operators as they navigate and interact with the global capital markets and the financial system. The Company manages, operates, and provides its products and services in three business segments: Capital Access Platforms, Financial Technology and Market Services. The Company's Capital Access Platforms segment includes Data & Listing Services, Index and Workflow & Insights. The Company's Financial Technology segment comprises of Financial Crime Management Technology, Regulatory Technology and Capital Markets Technology solutions. The Company's Market Services segment operates multiple exchanges and other marketplace facilities across several asset classes, including derivatives, commodities, cash equity, debt, structured products, and exchange-traded products (ETPs). The Market Services segment also provides clearing, settlement, and central depository services.

Executive Summary:

Nasdaq, Inc. (Ticker: NDAQ) represents a compelling investment opportunity within the financial services industry, particularly in the global exchange sector. NDAQ operates one of the most prominent stock exchanges globally and provides a range of financial products and services, including market technology, data analytics, and clearinghouse solutions. Despite facing market volatility and macroeconomic headwinds, Nasdaq's diversified revenue streams, continued growth in market services, and strategic acquisitions position the company well for future growth. At the same time, technical indicators suggest a favorable entry point for long-term investors.

Market Overview:

Nasdaq operates within the Financial Services sector and competes with other global exchanges, such as Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) and CME Group (CME). As of early August 2023, the broader financial services industry has experienced mixed performance, largely due to macroeconomic uncertainties such as rising interest rates, inflation, and potential recession risks. However, within the sector, exchanges and market services have remained resilient due to sustained trading activity, even amid volatility.

While NDAQ's performance slightly lags its competitors, the company’s year-over-year revenue growth of 4% (Q2 2023) suggests a stable upward trend. Nasdaq’s diversified revenue streams, which include listings, information services, and technology solutions, are less sensitive to trading volume declines than its competitors, making it a relatively safer play.

Key Growth Drivers:

A. Diversification of Revenue Streams: Nasdaq’s business model has become increasingly diversified. While historically reliant on market services (trading and listing fees), the company now generates approximately 70% of its revenues from non-trading sources, including analytics, data services, and market technology solutions. This diversification makes the company less susceptible to market volatility.

Market Technology: Nasdaq’s technology solutions are used by over 130 market operators globally. This segment grew by 7% year-over-year in Q2 2023, supported by increasing demand for trading platforms and technology infrastructure from international exchanges.

B. Expansion into ESG and Indexing Services: Nasdaq’s ESG initiatives and indexing business are crucial for future growth. As of 2023, Nasdaq manages more than 450 indices globally, including the popular Nasdaq-100, with assets under management in Nasdaq index-linked products surpassing $380 billion. Nasdaq’s ESG product suite includes tools for assessing corporate governance and sustainability practices, addressing the growing demand from investors focused on environmental and social factors.

Future Growth Catalysts:

A. Growth in Data Analytics and AI Integration: Nasdaq is investing heavily in expanding its data analytics capabilities. The company’s recent investments in AI-driven solutions aim to enhance its trading analytics and market surveillance systems. AI integration can drive higher demand for its data products, particularly among institutional investors and hedge funds.

B. Strategic Acquisitions: Nasdaq’s acquisition strategy is focused on enhancing its technology offerings. For example, in June 2023, Nasdaq announced the $10.5 billion acquisition of Adenza, a company that provides software solutions for risk management and regulatory compliance to financial institutions. This acquisition is expected to close in Q4 2023 and will significantly boost Nasdaq's regulatory and compliance technology segment.

Rationale and Justification:

Financials:Nasdaq's revenue for the latest reported period was $6.6 billion, with a gross profit of $4.3 billion and an operating income of $1.9 billion. The net income stood at $947 million, reflecting the company's ability to generate significant profits from its diversified revenue streams. The operating profit margin for Nasdaq is 0.14%, which, although relatively low, is consistent with the financial services sector where margins can be thin due to high operating costs. However, the company's asset utilization ratio of 18.77% indicates that Nasdaq is efficient in generating revenue from its assets. Nasdaq also had a 29% increase in Index business revenue and a 17% growth in SaaS revenue. The integration of the Adenza acquisition has also contributed significantly to the company's performance, with debt paydown progressing faster than planned and leverage reduced to 3.9x.

Stock Valuation: Nasdaq is trading at a P/E ratio of approximately 21.5x. This is slightly above the sector median but justified by its higher growth potential due to technological advancements and acquisitions. Despite the high debt levels, Nasdaq has a cash and short-term investments balance of $590 million, which, while not overly robust, is sufficient to cover short-term obligations. The company's interest coverage ratio and other solvency metrics suggest that while there are risks, Nasdaq is managing its debt effectively, with leverage reduced to 3.9x as of the latest report.

Technical Indicators:

  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): Nasdaq’s RSI stands at 48, indicating the stock is neither overbought nor oversold, suggesting it is trading near fair value and providing a solid entry point.
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): The MACD line has recently crossed above the signal line, which is typically seen as a bullish sign for short-term price momentum.
  • Cash Flow Generation: Nasdaq generated $289 million in operating cash flow in Q2 2023, providing a healthy cushion for further investments and shareholder returns.

Risks:

  • Low Solvency Score: Nasdaq's solvency score is 37 out of 100, indicating some concerns regarding its ability to meet long-term debt obligations. This score is influenced by the company's debt levels, with long-term debt standing at $9.2 billion and total debt obligations of $11.5 billion.
  • Mixed Signals: According to Macroaxis, Nasdaq's real value is estimated at $71.34 per share, which is slightly below the current market price of $73.01. This suggests that the stock might be slightly overvalued but still within a reasonable range. The target price from analysts averages at $71.25, with a range from $53.53 to $89.25, indicating a mixed outlook but generally supportive of the current valuation.

Conclusion:

Nasdaq, Inc. presents a well-rounded investment opportunity, driven by its diversified revenue model, strategic acquisitions, and commitment to technological innovation. The company’s strong financials, alongside favorable technical indicators, make it an attractive stock for long-term investors seeking exposure to the financial services sector. However, investors should also remain cautious of macroeconomic and regulatory risks. In light of these factors, Nasdaq is well-positioned for sustained growth and value creation, particularly through its non-trading revenue segments and advancements in technology.

Given the stock’s current valuation and strong future prospects, NDAQ remains a buy recommendation for investors seeking steady long-term gains.
